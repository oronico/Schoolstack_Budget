CRITICAL
Let's diversify away from philanthropy over time
Grants are wonderful but cyclical — shifting toward tuition or per-pupil revenue over a few years lets philanthropy stay
supplemental rather than foundational, and the model holds up if a single grant cycle slips.
Evidence: Philanthropy % (Y1): 64.5%  |  Philanthropy revenue: $266,667  |  Target: Under 20%
Mitigation: Build a transition plan to shift toward earned revenue (tuition, fees, per-pupil funding) so that philanthropy
becomes supplemental rather than foundational. Aim for under 20% by Year 3. Next step: Open Step 5: Revenue and either
grow tuition / per-pupil lines, or wind down a grant line by Year 3, until philanthropy is below 20% of Year 3 revenue.
HIGH
Revenue concentration
65% of revenue depends on grants and donations, which are inherently unpredictable. A focused revenue model is fine —
but it should be anchored to dependable, demand-driven income, not fundraising.
Evidence: revenue concentration: Needs attention
Mitigation: Strengthen enrollment pipeline evidence (waitlist, LOIs, retention data) and ensure revenue is anchored to
demand-driven income rather than uncertain grants.
MEDIUM
Let's back the enrollment ramp with evidence
Enrollment is the engine of every revenue and staffing assumption downstream. The more evidence we can attach to each
year's number, the more confident the rest of the model becomes.
Evidence: Year 1: 20 students  |  Year 2: 26 students  |  Year 3: 32 students  |  Year 4: 37 students  |  Year 5: 40 students  |  Capacity: 45
Mitigation: Back every enrollment target with documented demand — signed letters of intent, waitlist depth, community
survey results, or recruitment pipeline data. Growth over 25% per year typically requires an exceptional recruitment engine
or facility expansion. Next step: Open Step 4: Enrollment and either soften the steepest year to a 15-25% jump, or paste
your waitlist count, signed letters of intent, and recruitment plan into the Story step so reviewers see the evidence.
MEDIUM
Let's tie new hires to enrollment milestones
Hiring slightly behind enrollment, rather than ahead of it, keeps fixed costs aligned with the revenue actually arriving. It
also gives you a clean answer to 'what triggers each new role'.
Evidence: Year 2 enrollment: 26 students  |  Year 2 staffing cost: $159,666
Mitigation: Align staffing additions to enrollment milestones. Hire when students are enrolled, not when you hope they
will be. Use part-time or contract roles to bridge gaps. Next step: Open Step 6: Staffing and tie each new role's start date to
a specific enrollment milestone (e.g. 'add when Y2 enrollment hits 60'), or push the role into a later year.
