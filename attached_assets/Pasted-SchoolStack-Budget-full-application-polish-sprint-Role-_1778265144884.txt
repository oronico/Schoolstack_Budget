SchoolStack Budget full application polish sprint.

Role:
You are acting as product engineer, CFO-modeling QA lead, and founder-experience designer.

Goal:
Go through the full SchoolStack Budget application, not just the guest wizard, and make sure the experience is polished, logical, beginner-friendly, and robust enough for experienced operators to build a full-scale CFO-quality school financial model.

Product thesis:
SchoolStack Budget is for schools first. It helps even inexperienced entrepreneurs learn budgeting, accounting, and financial modeling in a safe, welcoming space. If a founder later wants to borrow money, apply for grants, communicate with a board, negotiate with a landlord, or talk to funders, the model should be credible enough to share.

Do not make the product feel like a bank portal.
Do not build a loan origination system.
Do not lead with underwriting judgment.
Do not make founders feel rejected or inadequate.

The app should feel:
- clear
- warm
- serious
- educational
- empowering
- accurate
- CFO-quality when needed
- nonjudgmental for beginners

Current architecture:
The authenticated wizard currently includes:
- Story
- School Details
- Actuals Intake for operating schools
- Enrollment
- Revenue
- Staffing
- Expenses
- Capital & Financing
- Assumptions & Sensitivity
- Review
- Consultant
- Lender Narrative
- Export

Use this structure. Do not rebuild from scratch.

PHASE 1. Information architecture review

Review the full wizard flow and confirm each step answers one clear question:

1. Story
Question: What kind of school are you building and why?

2. School Details
Question: What is the operating context, entity structure, model type, facility status, and planning stage?

3. Actuals Intake
Question: If you are already operating, what actually happened financially and operationally last year?

4. Enrollment
Question: How many students will you serve, and how confident are those numbers?

5. Revenue
Question: Where will money come from, how much, and when will it arrive?

6. Staffing
Question: Who does the work, what does it cost, and when do roles come online?

7. Expenses
Question: What does it cost to operate the school beyond staff?

8. Capital & Financing
Question: What startup capital, debt, or major financing assumptions affect the plan?

9. Assumptions & Sensitivity
Question: Which assumptions drive the model, and what happens if they change?

10. Review
Question: Does the model make sense mathematically and operationally?

11. Consultant
Question: What should the founder understand, fix, or explain before sharing the model?

12. Lender Narrative
Question: How can the founder explain the model credibly to an external audience?

13. Export
Question: Which deliverable does the founder need?

Acceptance:
- Every step has a clear purpose.
- No step feels duplicative.
- Beginner users understand why the step matters.
- Expert users can move efficiently without being slowed down by excessive coaching.
- The wizard should not feel like a loan application.

Add or strengthen a guidance mode toggle with at least two visible modes:

A. Guided Builder
For beginners.
Includes:

- examples

B. CFO Mode
For experienced operators.

Acceptance:

PHASE 4. Actuals-first pathway for operating schools

Operating schools should start with actuals.

Review Actuals Intake and strengthen it so it captures:
- prior-year enrollment
- prior-year revenue by category
- prior-year expenses by category
- ending cash
- tuition revenue actually collected
- public funding actually received
- philanthropy actually received
- personnel expense
- facility expense
- other operating expenses
- existing debt
- current cash
- profit and loss upload, if available
- accounting basis: cash, accrual, not sure

Then seed Year 1 projections from actuals where appropriate.

Add clear language:
“Your actuals are the best starting point. We will use them to help build a realistic projection, but you can adjust assumptions for growth, pricing, staffing, or facility changes.”

Acceptance:
- Operating schools are not treated like startups.
- Actuals clearly influence projections.
- The model distinguishes actuals from assumptions.
- The export shows what is actual and what is projected.

PHASE 5. Assumptions-first pathway for new schools

New schools should not feel punished for not having actuals.

For launch-stage founders, ask:
- projected opening month
- Year 1 operating months
- committed students
- signed agreements
- deposits collected
- waitlist
- tuition or public funding assumption
- facility status
- startup costs
- pre-opening cash needs
- first month with revenue
- first month with payroll
- first month with rent

Add language:
“Since you do not have actuals yet, we are building a planning model from assumptions. The stronger the evidence behind each assumption, the stronger and more useful your model becomes.”

Acceptance:
- New founders learn how to build defensible assumptions.
- The model does not pretend projections are actuals.
- Export includes an assumptions confidence section.
