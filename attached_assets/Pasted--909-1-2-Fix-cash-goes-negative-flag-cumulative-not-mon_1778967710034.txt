#909 - 1.2 — Fix cash_goes_negative flag (cumulative, not monthly)
What & Why
Oakwood Health Dimensions claims "Cash goes negative in month 1" even though cumulative cash stays positive every month of Y1 (lowest = $4,932 in July). The liquidity flag was triggering on monthly net cash flow < 0 and misreading the canonical cashRunwayMonths coverage ratio as a depletion-month index. It should trigger on min(cumulative_cash_by_month) < 0 and use buffer-thin copy when the trough is positive-but-slim.

The engine-side fix and the synthetic guardrail test are already landed (see "Already done" below). What's left is the real-seed verification on Oakwood, which the previous attempt got stuck on by trying to hand-roll a PDF text extractor in tests/dbg-board.ts. That path is a dead end — the packet builder feeds generateHealthSignals directly, so the liquidity signal's explanation is available as a plain string from buildPacketData / buildLenderPacket / buildBoardPacket output. No PDF rendering or text extraction is required to assert the copy on the real Oakwood seed.

explanation does NOT match /goes negative/i.
