Scholarship discount sign is flipped in V2. The Assumptions sheet stores it correctly as -$1,050 per student. But by the time it reaches Tuition & Funding, Budget Detail, and the row totals, it appears as a positive $105,000 line item that gets added to revenue instead of subtracted. The GRAND TOTAL REVENUE formula is =SUM(B7,B10,B13,B17,B20), where B10 is "Total Tuition Offsets." Revenue is overstated by $210K in Year 1, growing to $473K in Year 5.
Revenue logic double-counts tuition and ESA. The model treats $10,500 tuition per student and $8,700 ESA per student as additive, giving $19,200 per student before fees or philanthropy. In Florida, the Family Empowerment Scholarship pays the school in lieu of tuition, not on top of it. This is the main reason Year 5 net margins land at 50-70% (V2) and 50%+ (Lender Pro Forma), which is implausible for a private school.
The same school shows four different Year 1 revenue numbers across V2's tabs. Tuition & Funding, Budget Detail, and Budget Summary use formulas and produce $2,185,000. The 5-Year Operating Statement, Monthly Cash Flow, DSCR & Covenants, Lender Snapshot, and Scenarios all show $1,922,500 as hardcoded values. Financial Health shows $1,992,500 (matching the Lender Pro Forma engine instead). Net Income / Change in Net Assets has the same problem: $999,763 / $711,549 / $737,264 / $762,264 depending on the sheet. The downstream summary tabs an underwriter would open first show numbers that no longer match the budget driving them.
Lender Pro Forma's Summary tab has a Year 5 Net Margin of 0%. The formula in Summary!C13 points to '5-Year P&L'!G21, which is empty. Net Income is in G22. Easy one-cell fix.
Lender Pro Forma subtracts principal from Net Income. '5-Year P&L'!B22 = NOI - Interest - Principal & Capital Outlays. Principal repayment is a balance sheet item, not a P&L expense. Net Income should subtract interest only.
V2 staffing doesn't scale with enrollment. Teachers stay at 6 FTE while students grow from 100 to 200 (students-per-FTE rises from 7.7 to 15.4). Lender Pro Forma correctly uses =CEILING(Enrollment/Students_per_Teacher,1) and grows teachers from 10 to 19. This is the largest single driver of V2's implausibly high Year 5 margin.
Large swaths of summary metrics are hardcoded values instead of formulas. If Heritage Academy edits an assumption like enrollment, Budget Summary will update; DSCR & Covenants, Lender Snapshot, Financial Health, Scenarios, and Stress Tests will not. For a model that drives borrower-facing PDFs and lender packets, this is an integrity problem.
Single-Year Pro Forma has broken sample data. Personnel sums to $21.3M for a 100-student school because annual figures appear to be entered in monthly cells. Currently unusable as a demo.
Smaller items: Per-line escalation % inputs in V2 Assumptions (showing 0%) are not actually used; the model uses the global 3% rate. The "Capacity ≥ 7500%" covenant in DSCR & Covenants is a units typo (intended to be 75%). The Lender Pro Forma's interest schedule is hardcoded rather than using CUMIPMT like V2 does.
