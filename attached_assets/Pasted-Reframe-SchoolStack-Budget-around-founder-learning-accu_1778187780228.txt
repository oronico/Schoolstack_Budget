Reframe SchoolStack Budget around founder learning, accurate modeling, and lender-grade deliverables.

Product principle:
SchoolStack Budget is for school founders first. It should help even inexperienced entrepreneurs learn financial accounting and budgeting in a safe, welcoming way. The product should produce accurate, credible outputs that can later support lender, funder, board, landlord, or partner conversations.

Do not make the product feel like a bank portal.
Do not build a loan origination system.
Do not lead with underwriting judgment.
Do not make founders feel rejected or inadequate.

The experience should feel:
- clear
- warm
- serious
- educational
- empowering
- accurate
- nonjudgmental

Core user journey:
1. Founder learns the concept.
2. Founder enters their best available data.
3. If actuals exist, founder enters actuals.
4. If actuals do not exist, founder builds reasonable assumptions.
5. The tool checks the model for accuracy, gaps, and fragile assumptions.
6. The founder receives plain-English coaching.
7. The founder exports a lender-grade, funder-ready, board-ready model.

Required product language:
Use:
- “Let’s build this together.”
- “Here’s what this means.”
- “This assumption may need support.”
- “A lender or funder may ask about this.”
- “Your model gets stronger when...”
- “This is worth revisiting before you share the model externally.”
- “This does not mean your school is weak. It means this part of the plan needs more clarity.”

Avoid:
- approved
- declined
- ineligible
- failed
- underwriting decision
- credit decision
- bank determination
- pass/fail unless used only for internal tests

Next sprint priorities:

1. Beginner-friendly learning layer
Add short explanations near each major input:
- What is revenue?
- What is an expense?
- What is net income?
- What is cash flow?
- Why does timing matter?
- What is break-even enrollment?
- What are reserves?
- What is debt service?
- Why should the founder pay themselves eventually?

Each explanation should be plain English and no more than 2-4 sentences.

2. Actuals-first pathway for operating schools
If the school is already operating, ask:
- Do you have actual revenue and expenses from last year?
- What was your ending cash?
- What was actual enrollment?
- What revenue sources did you actually receive?
- What expenses did you actually pay?
- Do you have a profit and loss statement?

Then use actuals as the starting point for projections.

3. Assumptions-first pathway for new schools
If the school is launching, ask:
- How many students are realistically committed?
- What tuition or public funding is expected?
- When will money actually arrive?
- What must be paid before school starts?
- What expenses are fixed no matter how many students enroll?
- What expenses grow with students?

Make clear:
“Since you do not have actuals yet, we are building a planning model from assumptions. The stronger the evidence behind each assumption, the stronger the model.”

4. Model accuracy checks
Add founder-friendly checks:
- Revenue seems high for the number of students.
- Staffing may be too low for the number of students.
- Facility cost may be high relative to revenue.
- Founder compensation is missing.
- Cash goes negative in one or more months.
- Enrollment growth may need more evidence.
- Public funding timing may create a cash gap.
- Donations are carrying the model.

Frame these as coaching, not rejection.

5. Cash flow as a core teaching feature
Add or strengthen monthly cash flow:
- beginning cash
- cash received
- cash paid
- ending cash
- lowest cash month
- months below zero
- summer gap
- timing of tuition, ESA, voucher, or grant payments

Plain-English output:
“Your annual budget may look balanced, but cash gets tight in September because expenses arrive before revenue.”

6. Founder compensation teaching
Add a section:
“Paying yourself matters.”

Explain:
- Many founders start unpaid.
- That may be realistic temporarily.
- A sustainable school eventually needs to pay the people doing the work.
- The model should show when founder compensation begins.

Show two views:
- Your current model
- Your model with founder compensation included

7. Lender-grade outputs, but school-facing labels
Export outputs should be named:
- 1-Year Operating Budget
- 5-Year Financial Model
- Founder Planning Workbook
- Board and Funder Summary
- Lender Conversation Snapshot

Do not call founder-facing exports:
- credit memo
- underwriting file
- approval packet
- bank review

8. Assumption quality layer
For key assumptions, ask:
- Is this based on actuals?
- Is this based on a signed agreement?
- Is this based on a quote?
- Is this based on research?
- Is this an estimate?

Use this to create an “Assumptions Confidence” section in the export.

9. Plain-English summary page
Every model should generate:
- What your model says
- What looks strong
- What needs more clarity
- What could create cash pressure
- What to fix first
- What someone reviewing this may ask

10. Guardrail
Every risk flag must include a next step.

Bad:
“Staffing costs are too high.”

Better:
“Staffing is 72% of revenue. That may leave too little room for rent, curriculum, and reserves. Consider phasing in one role later or increasing enrollment before hiring.”
