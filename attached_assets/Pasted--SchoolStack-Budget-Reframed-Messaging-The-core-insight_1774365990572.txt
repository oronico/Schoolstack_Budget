# SchoolStack Budget: Reframed Messaging
## The core insight: most users have zero business experience

---

## THE SHIFT

**Before (lender-first):** "Build a financial model your lender will take seriously."
**After (educator-first):** "The budget tool for people who didn't go to business school."

The old framing assumed users wanted capital. Most don't, at least not yet. What they want is to stop feeling lost. They opened a school because they're great educators, and now they're drowning in financial decisions nobody prepared them for. Budget is their CFO, their business partner, and their teacher all at once.

The Lending Lab readiness feature is still there. It's a powerful bonus for the subset pursuing capital. But it's Step 7 in the wizard, not the headline.

## THE VOICE RULE

Every piece of Budget copy should pass this test:

"Would a former teacher who just opened a 30-student microschool
feel WELCOMED or INTIMIDATED by this sentence?"

If intimidated, rewrite it. Always.

Terms to avoid in primary copy: underwriting, debt service coverage ratio,
DSCR, pro forma (use "financial model" or "budget"), net margin (use
"whether you break even"), revenue per pupil (use "revenue per student").

Terms that are fine: budget, expenses, revenue, payroll, rent, cash on hand
(explain as "cash runway"), break-even, healthy/at risk.

The technical terms (DSCR, net margin, etc.) belong in the CFO Consultant
step and the export documents, where users have opted into the detail.
They do NOT belong in the landing page, onboarding, or marketing.
