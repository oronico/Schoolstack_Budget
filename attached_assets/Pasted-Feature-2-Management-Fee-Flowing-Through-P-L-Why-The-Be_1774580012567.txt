Feature 2: Management Fee Flowing Through P&L
Why: The Bellwether model treats the charter management fee (13% of revenue) as a first-class expense line item. SchoolStack has hasManagementFee and managementFeePercent on the school profile, and an authorizer_fee expense row in the defaults — but the fee is buried as a generic admin expense and doesn't appear prominently in exports or consultant analysis.
Current state:

schoolProfile.hasManagementFee (boolean) and managementFeePercent (number) exist in schema
ExpenseStep.tsx creates/updates an authorizer_fee row with percent_of_revenue driver
The fee shows on the Assumptions tab of exports as a text note
It does NOT appear as a named line in the P&L section of exports
Consultant engine doesn't analyze it

Changes Required
This is mostly already wired. The authorizer_fee expense row exists and flows through computeExpenseForYear() like any other row. The gaps are:

Visibility in exports: The authorizer_fee row gets grouped under "Administrative / General" in exports. It should be called out separately in the P&L summary — add a line between operating expenses and net income: "Less: Authorizer / Management Fee" with the computed value. This matches the Bellwether model's treatment (line 31 of School roll-up: "Charter Management Fee" as a distinct cost line).
